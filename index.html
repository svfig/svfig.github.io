Meeting Schedule
=========

10:00 --- Coffee and a Chat

==========

10:30 --- Ho-hum : Quantum Computing Becomes Routine --- Jack J. Woehr
"We're still years off from a generally marketable quantum computer, but it's obvious that it's really going to happen.  Explore resources on the net that let you get started with quantum computing right now."

==========

11:30 --- Lunch
We'll have lunch at The Treehouse.
http://treehousestanford.com/

==========

13:00 --- Non-Coding DNA in c. Ruddii --- C. H. Ting
"Ruddii is the smallest free-living protobacterium, with 173802 bases. 227 genes were identifies. 40 sequences of 14764 bases are non-coding DNA. I hope to find how a cell computer is programmed in these non-coding DNA. I wrote a pattern matching program to identify repeated patterns in these sequences. There are interest features I observed, but so far, no smoking gun was found. I will show you the progress I made, and the problems I have."

==========

14:00 --- Break ---

==========

14:20 --- Port of the Sockpuppet API to the Cortex-M0 --- Robert Sexton
"I'll demonstrate MPE Forth Lite running under WINE and compile binaries that can be installed with the built-in DFU boot loader onto the STMF072B discovery board.  The Forth prompt is available via a USB CDC interface without any special dongles."

==========

15:20 --- Random Access
We always find something to talk about.  We may look at video from Forth Day or the Apollo Program onboard computer.


==========

16:00 --- CLEAN UP AND ADJOURN
