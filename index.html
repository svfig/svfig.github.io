Meeting Schedule
=========

8:00 --- Coffee and a Chat

=========

8:30 --- Chairman's Welcome --- George Perry

=========

8:35 --- Technical Archeology Or Why I Like Paper --- Bill Ragsdale
"I have a brief talk I'm doing for Hackers involving reconstructing Forth work archived from 1980. The basis in recovering a Forth project."

=========

8:45 --- Gforth Under RTEMS  --- John Harbold
John will review his progress and discuss the challenges of this pathway to portable, embedded Forth.
https://www.rtems.org/

=========

8:55 --- The Evolution of the Forth Engine --- C.H. Ting
 
=========

9:15 --- gelFORTH - Interactive Digital and Analog Hardware Design --- Andreas Wagner
An umbilical Forth, bootstrapped over the KitProg SWD programmer/debugger. Compiles Forth to the UDB array (the Cypress PSoC 5LP CPLD) during operation for interactive hardware design. Works with 100% Free & Open Source Software.https://hackaday.io/project/12488-gelforth
http://www.forth.org/fd/FD-V21N1,2.pdf#page=21 http://www.forth.org/fd/FD-V21N1,2.pdf

=========

9:35 --- Spreadsheet Forth --- Joseph O'Connor
Creole Forth for Excel is a simple scripting language implemented with spreadsheets for its data structures. It can be extended with either low-level primitives in VBA or with high-level (colon) definitions.
https://github.com/tiluser/Creole-Forth-For-Excelhttps://www.reddit.com/r/programming/comments/54b5fk/creoleforthforexcel_forth_in_a_spreadsheet/

=========

10:05 --- Break
We'll be ordering lunch about this time, be sure to have your order in!

=========

10:15 --- Adding an SNMP Agent to a SwiftForth Application --- Leon Wagner
FORTH, Inc. recently added an SNMP agent to a SwiftForth for Windows application using the WinSNMP library. Leon will show the overall SNMP agent design and will focus on the library interface, which is common to SwiftForth on the Windows, Linux, and macOS platforms.

=========

10:45 --- Back; and Forth Across the Pond --- Ken Boak
Ken will provide a look at some of the new hardware and software Forth related developments happening in the UK and Europe.

==========

11:03 --- An Open Source Toolchain--- James Bowman
Following on Ken's talk,  James will be providing more detailed  information about one specific hardware development - highlighting it's applications to an open source Forth, running on open source hardware - produced entirely using an open source toolchain with an end-of-presentation demo.

==========

11:21 --- eForth 1.0 on Kestrel-3 - it works! --- Samuel A. Falvo II
A *very* fast demo of eForth booting and doing something
significant.  However, the compiler is *really* slow -- much slower
than anticipated.  Want to help Kestrel Computer Project?  Would love
to get some help speeding up the compiler!

==========

11:30 --- Defining Defining Words, A New Old Syntax --- Bill RagsdaleFirst proposed in 1980, this syntax clarifies defining words and eases target compiling. Lost in the fog of time, Bill has updated it to contemporary practice.

==========
